Why Founders and Coders?

I initially considered joining FAC in hopes to be a well rounded, strong developer by evolving my programming skills. My consideration gravitated to commitment after joining the weekly meet-ups (both in person and remote). I found that when attending the meet-ups my skills were always improving or challenged. The attentiveness on pair-programming allowed me learn from the other students as well as share my thoughts and ideas. The approach that was taken was the “driver” and “navigator” approach where the driver was writing the code and the navigator reviewed/questioned the code being written. Additionally, FAC’s peer lead approach will improve my confidence from the transition from the prerequisites to the course as the mentors are graduates and they have has the time to reflect and improve on what they have previously been taught and encourage collaborative learning, as students we can reap the rewards of their newly gained knowledge. Also, I appreciated the importance of FAC’s prerequisites as I was given a chance to feel out the waters for HTML, CSS & JavaScript and see if I was able to follow through and accomplish the challenges I came across. Throughout the process of completing my FAC application I realised my strengths were in repeating techniques until I understood what and why I was using this method or that statement, I also found that Google was my best friend when I was facing difficult obstacles. Overall, I think that FAC is the right place for me to begin my programming career because of the level of preparation it takes to join the course, the method of teaching and my own experience when participating in the meet-ups.

What about Programming?
